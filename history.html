The Historical Background of Dabbaso's Oromo Clan

History of Dabbaso

Dabbaso is a vibrant town located in the Sayo-Nole district, west Wollaga zone of the Oromia state in Ethiopia. Dabbaso is a clan name but according to the story told, they are not from among the other Oromo clans around that area. The Oromo clan living in the area are called the Noles (Nole torba meaning the seven Nole).

The History of the Town

Dabbaso was founded in the 18th century as a local market for weekly trading by the head of the Dabbaso clan called Qoro. The town's history is deeply rooted in the traditions and values of its people, who have worked tirelessly to build a better future for generations to come.

Timeline of Development

1980s: The Early Years

Dabbaso was a small village with limited infrastructure. The community relied on agriculture and traditional practices.

1990s: Growth and Expansion

The town began to grow, with the establishment of a primary school and a local market.

2000s: Modernization

Key infrastructure, such as a high school, police station, and polyclinic, was developed.

2020s: A Thriving Community

Dabbaso is now a bustling town with modern facilities, private clinics, and a strong sense of community.
